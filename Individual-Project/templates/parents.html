Parent:

Encourage independence: Help your child develop self-confidence and self-esteem by encouraging them to make their own decisions and take responsibility for their actions.

Teach coping strategies: Help your child learn how to say no to peer pressure and how to resist negative influences. This could include role-playing different scenarios or teaching them specific phrases they can use to decline.

Be a good role model: Show your child how to resist peer pressure by setting a good example. Avoid smoking, excessive drinking, and drugs, and discourage these activities in your child's presence.

Get involved: Stay informed about what your child is doing and who they are spending time with. Attend school events, parent-teacher conferences, and open houses to stay connected with their school community.

Provide a positive environment: Create a home environment that is safe, supportive, and positive. Encourage your child to participate in activities that they enjoy and that build self-esteem.

Seek professional help: If your child is struggling with peer pressure, seek the help of a counselor or therapist. They can work with your child to develop coping strategies and improve self-esteem.
